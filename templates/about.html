Predicting the Air Quality Index

Summary

This project leverages regression techniques to predict AQI based on various weather parameters. The system evaluates multiple models and selects the best one based on performance metrics such as: Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Mean Squared Error (MSE), and RÂ² score.

What is AQI?

Air Quality Index (AQI) is a measure used to communicate how polluted the air is.

Weather parameters play a crucial role in determining AQI

  • Precipitation: More rainfall generally improves air quality by washing away pollutants.
  • Temperature: Higher temperatures can worsen air quality by increasing pollution-related chemical reactions.
  • Wind Speed: Higher wind speeds improve air quality by dispersing pollutants.

AQI Interpretation:

  • 0-10: Good air quality, minimal pollution.
  • AQI>10: Air quality is unhealthy, precautions needed.
